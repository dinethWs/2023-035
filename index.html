Research Gap


Limited awareness and utilization of Bloom's Taxonomy among university students, which adversely affects their academic performance in standardized exams, and the proposed personalized self-learning system aims to address this gap by empowering students to align their study materials with Bloom's Taxonomy and enhance their cognitive skills for improved academic success.

Research Objective


Main Objective:
Develop an online platform for undergraduate students that enables them to upload their study materials and engage in effective self-study.

Sub Objectives:
1. Wijayasena W.D.N.D.T (IT20133368) LEADER:
Develop an effective recommendation system that provides personalized and contextually relevant resources during their self-study sessions.
2. Senaweera T.I.S (IT20123468):
Develop a method to predict student performance utilizing Bloom's Taxonomy levels integrated with Exponentially Weighted Moving Average (EWMA).
3. Weerasekara N.N (IT20133504):
Develop a knowledge-based system that uses domain-specific knowledge to generate more precise and significant mind maps from the input material.
4. Colambage K.G (IT20126438):
Develop a system to generate questions categorized according to Bloom's Taxonomy levels from uploaded study material with answers.
