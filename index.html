BloomQuest

A Personalized Learning Platform Based on Bloom's Taxonomy and Knowledge Based Concepts.



































Background and Introduction


This research delves into the academic challenges faced by university students, particularly the stress and pressure they experience. Students often struggle with tough subjects, assignments, and exams, and they may lack efficient study techniques. Additionally, many are unaware of how Bloom's Taxonomy influences their exams. By examining these issues, this research seeks to uncover the extent of these challenges and find ways to help students better manage academic stress, improve study methods, and grasp the impact of Bloom's Taxonomy on their exams, ultimately enhancing their academic experience.





Research Gap


Absence of personalized self-learning systems that specifically guide university students in understanding and effectively applying Bloom's Taxonomy in their self-study practices. Current educational resources do not adequately address this gap, indicating the need for innovative solutions that empower students to optimize their self-study efforts and enhance their academic success.





Research Problem


Deficiency in effective, personalized systems that assist university students in developing self-study skills and aligning their learning materials with Bloom's Taxonomy. This lack of comprehensive support has been associated with a negative impact on students' academic performance in standardized exams.





Research Solution


BloomQuest is an innovative approach that incorporates Bloom's Taxonomy, enabling students to identify their cognitive levels and develop essential learning skills with features like:
1. Performance tracking and insights for each module.
2. Mind map generation based on student-uploaded study materials as the foundation.
3. Advanced search functionality for extended learning.
4. Quizzes based on Bloom's Taxonomy.
This system empowers students to take control of their learning, fostering independence and deeper understanding.





Research Objectives


Main Objective:
Develop an online platform for undergraduate students that enables them to upload their study materials and engage in effective self-study.

Sub Objectives:
1. Wijayasena W.D.N.D.T (IT20133368) LEADER:
Develop an effective recommendation system that provides personalized and contextually relevant resources during their self-study sessions.

2. Senaweera T.I.S (IT20123468):
Develop a method to predict student performance utilizing Bloom's Taxonomy levels integrated with Exponentially Weighted Moving Average (EWMA).

3. Weerasekara N.N (IT20133504):
Develop a knowledge-based system that uses domain-specific knowledge to generate more precise and significant mind maps from the input material.

4. Colambage K.G (IT20126438):
Develop a system to generate questions categorized according to Bloom's Taxonomy levels from uploaded study material with answers.
